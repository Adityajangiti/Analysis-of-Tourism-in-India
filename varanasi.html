VARANASI

Varanasi, also known as Kashi, is a city in northern India that is considered one of the holiest places in Hinduism. It is situated on the banks of the River Ganges and is known for its ghats, temples, and spiritual aura. Here are some places to visit in Varanasi:

  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple - One of the most important Hindu temples in the world, the Kashi Vishwanath Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and attracts devotees from all over the world.
  • Dashashwamedh Ghat - One of the most popular and vibrant ghats in Varanasi, Dashashwamedh Ghat is a great place to witness the evening Ganga Aarti and to take a boat ride on the Ganges.
  • Sarnath - A famous Buddhist pilgrimage site located just a few kilometers away from Varanasi, Sarnath is known for its ancient stupas, temples, and monasteries.
  • Manikarnika Ghat - One of the most sacred ghats in Varanasi, Manikarnika Ghat is where the final rites of the dead are performed. It is considered an auspicious place for Hindus to be cremated.
  • Banaras Hindu University - A renowned university that is home to many prestigious schools and colleges. It has a sprawling campus with beautiful gardens, museums, and temples.
  • Ramnagar Fort - A magnificent fort located on the banks of the River Ganges, Ramnagar Fort is known for its impressive architecture and museum that houses a collection of vintage cars, weapons, and paintings.
  • Assi Ghat - A popular ghat in Varanasi, Assi Ghat is a great place to witness the sunrise and to take a boat ride on the Ganges.
  • Tulsi Manas Temple - A beautiful temple dedicated to Lord Rama, Tulsi Manas Temple is known for its intricate carvings and murals that depict the Ramayana.
  • Alamgir Mosque - A beautiful mosque that is considered one of the finest examples of Mughal architecture in Varanasi. It is situated on the banks of the Ganges and offers stunning views of the river and the city.
  • Chunar Fort - A historic fort located near Varanasi, Chunar Fort is known for its strategic location and impressive architecture. It is a great place to explore for history buffs and adventure seekers.


    • LUGGAGES TO CARRY

    When packing for a Varanasi tour, it is important to consider the weather, culture, and activities you plan to do during your stay. Here are some essential items that you may want to include in your luggage:

  • Clothing: Lightweight and breathable clothing is recommended for Varanasi, as the city can get very hot and humid. It is also recommended to cover your shoulders and knees, especially when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Footwear: Comfortable and sturdy shoes are a must for walking around Varanasi, as the streets can be uneven and crowded. Sandals or flip-flops are also recommended for visiting temples and taking a dip in the Ganges.
  • Sun protection: Sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat or scarf to cover your head are essential to protect yourself from the strong sun.
  • Insect repellent: Mosquitoes are common in Varanasi, especially during the monsoon season, so it is advisable to carry an insect repellent.
  • Personal hygiene items: Hand sanitizer, wet wipes, and toilet paper are recommended as some public restrooms in Varanasi may not provide these items.
  • Medications: Any medications that you require should be carried along with the prescriptions. It is also advisable to carry a first-aid kit for minor injuries.
  • Camera: Varanasi is a beautiful and photogenic city, so don't forget to bring a camera to capture all the wonderful sights.
  • Cash: Although there are ATMs and banks in Varanasi, it is advisable to carry cash in small denominations for local purchases and offerings at religious sites.
  • Backpack or day bag: A small backpack or day bag is useful for carrying essential items during sightseeing tours.
  • Water bottle: It is important to stay hydrated in the hot and humid weather, so carry a refillable water bottle to avoid buying bottled water.
