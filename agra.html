AGRA

Agra is a city in northern India and home to one of the most famous tourist attractions in the world, the Taj Mahal. Here are some other popular tourist places to visit in Agra:

  • Taj Mahal - This beautiful white marble mausoleum, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, is one of the most famous tourist attractions in the world.
  • Agra Fort - This 16th-century fortress, also known as the Red Fort of Agra, was the main residence of the Mughal emperors until the capital was shifted to Delhi.
  • Fatehpur Sikri - This abandoned city, built by Mughal Emperor Akbar in the 16th century, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a great place to explore.
  • Itmad-ud-Daulah's Tomb - Also known as the "Baby Taj," this beautiful tomb was built by Nur Jahan, the wife of Jahangir, for her father.
  • Mehtab Bagh - This beautiful garden complex, located across the Yamuna River from the Taj Mahal, offers a stunning view of the Taj Mahal at sunset.
  • Akbar's Tomb - This beautiful mausoleum, built for the Mughal Emperor Akbar, is a stunning example of Mughal architecture.
  • Jama Masjid - This beautiful mosque, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, is a great place to experience the local culture and architecture.
  • Chini ka Rauza - This beautiful tomb, built for the Persian poet and scholar Allama Afzal Khan Mullah, is known for its stunning blue and white tilework.

    • LUGGAGE TO CARRY

    If you are planning to visit Agra, here are some luggage items you may want to consider packing:

  • Clothing - Pack clothes that are suitable for the weather. Agra experiences hot and dry summers, so light and breathable clothes are recommended. In winter, temperatures can drop, so it's a good idea to pack some warmer clothes as well.
  • Footwear - Pack comfortable walking shoes as there are many tourist places in Agra that require walking. Flip flops or sandals are also a good idea for hot weather.
  • Sun protection - Pack sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat to protect yourself from the sun during the day.
  • Insect repellent - Agra is known for mosquitoes, so it's a good idea to pack insect repellent to protect yourself from mosquito bites.
  • Medications - If you take any prescription medication, make sure to bring enough for your entire trip. It's also a good idea to bring some basic over-the-counter medications like pain relievers, antacids, and anti-diarrheal medication.
  • Power bank and charger - Carry a portable charger and the necessary chargers for your electronics as you may need them during your trip.
  • Travel adapter - India uses type D and type C power plugs, so if you are traveling from a country that uses a different type of plug, you will need a travel adapter.
  • Backpack or daypack - A small backpack or daypack can be useful for carrying essential items like water, snacks, and a camera while exploring the city.
  • Money and documents - Make sure to carry enough cash or a credit/debit card, and keep your important documents like passport, visa, and ID card safe.
